# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Myself, primarily — a personal creative outlet for publishing thoughts on technology, design, and life. The blog exists because I want to write and share, not because there's an audience waiting. Secondary readers are fellow developers and designers who find their way here through posts or links and appreciate a space that feels personal rather than performative.

## Product Purpose

A personal blog that captures ideas without the pressure of being a publication. No SEO-optimized headlines, no content calendar, no "growth strategy." It succeeds when writing it feels good and the space reflects the person behind it — dark, atmospheric, and intentional. The design is part of the voice, not a wrapper around it.

## Brand Personality

Dark, atmospheric, confident, introspective. Not moody for melodrama — the dark is a canvas that makes the writing the light source. The tone is personal and direct, not corporate or clever. It's a room with the lights dimmed and a good lamp on.

## Anti-references

Explicitly not AI-slop: no glassmorphism, no gradient text, no generic card grids with icon + heading + body, no sketchy SVG illustrations, no stripe backgrounds, no side-stripe border accents, no numbered section markers as default scaffolding. Nothing that reads "generated." Every layout choice should feel earned, not borrowed from a template.

## Design Principles

1. **Content-first, decoration-second** — the writing is the event. Animations, particles, and donuts enhance rather than distract. If a visual element doesn't serve the reading experience, it doesn't belong.
2. **Dark with intention** — the dark palette sets atmosphere, not just contrast. Every dark choice is tested for readability: body text hits WCAG AA (4.5:1), accent draws the eye, surfaces have enough separation without relying on heavy borders.
3. **One voice, no pretension** — the blog sounds like a person, not a brand. Copy is direct and unpolished in the right ways. No "About" pages that read like a resume. No "Connect" that sounds like a sales pitch.
4. **Details over templates** — no section feels like it was dropped from a library. The hero's particle canvas, the ASCII donut in About Me, the marquee strip — each is chosen because it fits this specific voice, not because "landing pages do this."
5. **Motion as punctuation** — every animation has a reason: scrolling reveals the next thought, counters mark milestones, the donut spins because it's fun. If the animation doesn't clarify, orient, or delight, it's removed.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Body text hits minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio against backgrounds. Reduced motion supported — every animation respects `prefers-reduced-motion` with a static fallback. No information conveyed through color alone.
